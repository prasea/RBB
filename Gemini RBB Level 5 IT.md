## Topic 1: Financial Institutions in Nepal: Role, Contribution and Status

In Nepal, the financial system is structurally broadly categorized into two major divisions: **Banking Institutions** (regulated primarily by the Central Bank, Nepal Rastra Bank) and **Non-Bank Financial Institutions** (regulated by respective dedicated boards or authorities). They serve as the backbone of economic mobilization, financial inclusion, and secure digital transaction infrastructure.

---

### 1.1 Banking Institutions

Under the **Bank and Financial Institutions Act (BAFIA), 2073**, banking institutions in Nepal follow a strict four-tier classification system (Class A, B, C, and D), alongside a specialized Infrastructure Development Bank.

```
                       ┌────────────────────────────────────────┐
                       │  NRB Regulated Banking System (BAFIA)  │
                       └───────────────────┬────────────────────┘
                                           │
         ┌──────────────────┬──────────────┴─────┬─────────────────────┐
         ▼                  ▼                    ▼                     ▼
┌────────────────┐ ┌────────────────┐ ┌────────────────────┐ ┌───────────────────┐
│    Class A     │ │    Class B     │ │      Class C       │ │      Class D      │
│   Commercial   │ │  Development   │ │ Finance Companies  │ │   Microfinance    │
│     Banks      │ │     Banks      │ │                    │ │   Institutions    │
└────────────────┘ └────────────────┘ └────────────────────┘ └───────────────────┘

```

#### 1.1.1 Commercial Banks (Class "A")

* **Role & Core Functions:** Commercial banks form the bedrock of the economy. They accept all types of deposits (current, savings, fixed), provide working capital and term loans, issue letters of credit (LCs), process international trade transactions, and offer advanced digital banking facilities (RTGS, API integrations, internet/mobile banking).
* **Status & Contribution:** As the largest sector in the financial ecosystem, they dominate total assets and deposits. They drive large-scale corporate infrastructure funding and modern digital payment adoption. RBB itself is a leading state-owned Class "A" commercial bank.

#### 1.1.2 Development Banks (Class "B")

* **Role & Core Functions:** Divided into national-level and district-level operations. They focus on local economic development, agricultural productivity, small and medium enterprises (SMEs), and housing loans. While they perform basic banking functions, they are legally restricted from issuing certain trade instruments like industrial LCs.
* **Status & Contribution:** They bridge the gap between large commercial entities and grassroots micro-lending, providing substantial access to credit across mid-tier businesses in provincial regions.

#### 1.1.3 Finance Companies (Class "C")

* **Role & Core Functions:** Specialized financial institutions focusing primarily on consumer financing, hire-purchase (auto/machinery loans), housing finance, and term deposits.
* **Status & Contribution:** They provide higher flexibility for personal loans and asset financing. While their market share has consolidated due to strict NRB paid-up capital increments, they fulfill specialized consumer credit demands.

#### 1.1.4 Microfinance Institutions (Class "D")

* **Role & Core Functions:** Operate under a wholesale or retail lending model to serve the unbanked and rural poor. They offer collateral-free group loans, micro-savings, and financial literacy programs modeled heavily on the Grameen Bank blueprint.
* **Status & Contribution:** Vital for grassroots poverty reduction, women's empowerment, and small agri-businesses. They face current regulatory scrutiny regarding capping interest rates and avoiding multiple-banking lending traps.

#### 1.1.5 Infrastructure Development Bank (NIFRA)

* **Role & Core Functions:** A specialized financial institution established to invest exclusively in large-scale national infrastructure projects (hydropower, highways, airports, and smart cities). It raises funds via long-term bonds and foreign currencies but cannot accept individual retail deposits or extend generic consumer loans.
* **Status & Contribution:** Nepal Infrastructure Bank Limited (NIFRA) stands as the singular entity under this category, filling a vital gap in long-term project financing that traditional commercial banks struggle to hold on their balance sheets due to asset-liability mismatching.

---

### 1.2 Non-Bank Financial Institutions

These institutions mobilize long-term public contracts, contractual savings, and social protection funds. They do not hold traditional banking licenses under BAFIA but are core institutional investors driving the financial capital market.

#### 1.2.1 Insurance Companies

* **Role & Status:** Categorized cleanly into Life and Non-Life Insurance sectors, regulated strictly by the **Nepal Insurance Authority (Nepal Beema Pradhikaran)**.
* **Contribution:** They provide vital risk-mitigation channels for individuals and commercial properties. Crucially for banking, they act as major institutional depositors and provide credit insurance policies linked to bank loans.

#### 1.2.2 Karmachari Sanchaya Kosh (Employees' Provident Fund - EPF)

* **Role & Status:** A statutory body operating under the Ministry of Finance. It manages the compulsory retirement savings of civil servants, army personnel, police, and corporate employees across Nepal.
* **Contribution:** It manages massive asset portfolios. It offers specialized welfare loans to its contributors (housing, education) and behaves as a primary consortium investor in massive national projects (such as large hydropower setups).

#### 1.2.3 Nagarik Lagani Kosh (Citizen Investment Trust - CIT)

* **Role & Status:** Established under the Citizen Investment Trust Act, 2047, to operate capital markets, encourage public savings, and run retirement/investment schemes for both citizens and institutional clients.
* **Contribution:** Acts as a market maker in the Nepal Stock Exchange (NEPSE), manages mutual funds, operates citizen unit schemes, and provides institutional long-term loans.

#### 1.2.4 Samajik Surakshya Kosh (Social Security Fund - SSF)

* **Role & Status:** An institution born under the Social Security Act, 2074, targeted at formal and informal private sector workers.
* **Contribution:** Collects regular contributions from employer and employee to provide robust security packages (medical, maternity, accidental, and old-age pensions). It rapidly accumulates significant investable liquidity, which is heavily distributed back into the formal banking system as institutional fixed deposits.

### 2.1 Capital Fund

The Capital Fund is the financial cushion a bank must maintain to absorb unexpected credit, market, and operational losses without risking depositors' money. In Nepal, it is strictly governed by the **NRB Capital Adequacy Framework** (aligned with Basel III standards).

* **Tier 1 Capital (Core Capital):** The highest-quality, permanent capital. It consists of paid-up equity capital, statutory general reserves, share premium, and retained earnings.
* **Tier 2 Capital (Supplementary Capital):** Lower-quality capital. It includes general loan loss provisions (up to a specific cap), subordinated term debt, and exchange equalization reserves.
* **Regulatory Minimums:** For Class "A" Commercial Banks like RBB, NRB mandates a **Total Capital Fund of minimum 11%** of total Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA), with **Tier 1 Capital comprising at least 8.5%** (including capital conservation buffers).

### 2.2 Loan Loss Provisioning

Banks must set aside a portion of their profits as a provision to cover potential defaults from their loan portfolio. NRB Directive No. 2 dictates a strict classification of loans based on aging (overdue periods) and specifies provisioning rates.

The classification matrix includes:

* **Good / Pass Loans:** Principal and interest overdue up to 1 month. Provision: **1.3%** *(Note: NRB adjusts this periodically based on macroeconomic conditions).*
* **Watchlist:** Overdue between 1 to 3 months, or loans showing structural weaknesses. Provision: **5%**.
* **Sub-standard:** Overdue between 3 to 6 months. Provision: **25%**.
* **Doubtful:** Overdue between 6 to 12 months. Provision: **50%**.
* **Loss:** Overdue for more than 12 months, or loans with absolute deterioration of collateral security. Provision: **100%**.

> **Note:** "Pass" and "Watchlist" loans are classified as **Performing Loans**, while "Sub-standard", "Doubtful", and "Loss" are grouped as **Non-Performing Loans (NPL)**.

### 2.3 Credit-Deposit (CD) Ratio

The CD ratio measures how much of a bank's total mobilized deposits are deployed into productive loans. It acts as a fundamental liquidity metric to prevent banks from over-extending credit.


`CD Ratio = ( Total Credit Extended  / Total Deposit Accumulated ) * 100`

* **Current Cap:** NRB enforces a strict daily upper limit of **90%** on the CD ratio. If a bank breaches this ceiling, NRB imposes financial penalties. From an IT perspective, this requires real-time core banking system (CBS) compliance alerts.

### 2.4 Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)

The CRR is the percentage of total domestic deposits that a commercial bank must hold as a cash reserve in its account with Nepal Rastra Bank. It is a key monetary policy tool used to control system liquidity and inflation.

* **Current Requirement:** Commercial banks must maintain a minimum **4%** daily/weekly average CRR.
* **Key Distinction:** Unlike statutory liquidity ratio (SLR) assets, the funds held as CRR do not earn any interest.

### 2.5 Refinancing, Rescheduling, and Restructuring

When borrowers face financial difficulties, banks handle non-performing or stressed assets using three distinct regulatory options:

| Term                        | Operational Definition                                                                                                                                                                                  | Legal Status                                                |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Refinancing (पुनर्कर्जा)**    | Subsidized or targeted wholesale funding provided by NRB to commercial banks, allowing them to issue low-interest loans to prioritized sectors (such as hydropower, export industries, or agriculture). | Normal Regulatory Track                                     |
| **Rescheduling (पुनःतालिकीकरण)** | Modifying the repayment timeline of a loan by extending its maturity date without changing the underlying economic terms of the contract.                                                               | Stressed Asset (Requires 12.5% to 100% provision variation) |
| **Restructuring (पुनःसंरचना)**  | Altering the nature or terms of the loan contract, which may include lowering the interest rate, changing the type of credit facility, or waving past dues based on a viable recovery plan.             | Stressed Asset (Requires minimum 12.5% provision)           |

### 2.6 Base Rate

The Base Rate is the internal reference interest rate below which commercial banks are legally barred from lending, except for specific loans authorized by NRB. It ensures pricing transparency and protects banks from predatory pricing.

* **Components:** Cost of funds, Cost of maintaining CRR & SLR, Operational cost, and Return on Assets (ROA) margin component.
* **Formulaic Impact:** The final landing rate offered to a customer is calculated as: **$\text{Base Rate} + \text{Premium}$** (where premium depends on client risk rating).

### 2.7 Credit Information and Blacklisting

The **Credit Information Bureau (CIB) of Nepal** acts as a centralized data repository tracking the repayment histories of all bank borrowers.

* **Blacklisting Criteria:** If a borrower defaults on a loan exceeding a threshold set by NRB, or commits financial fraud (such as bouncing checks willfully), the lender submits their details to the CIB for blacklisting.
* **Systemic Effect:** Blacklisted individuals or companies lose access to new loans, checking accounts, and debit/credit privileges across all financial institutions in Nepal.

### 2.8 Credit Ratings

Credit rating refers to the independent evaluation of an entity's or a debt instrument's creditworthiness by an accredited rating agency (such as ICRA Nepal or Care Ratings Nepal).

* **Application:** NRB mandates that corporate borrowers seeking large loan facilities (typically 50 Crore NPR and above) must obtain an official credit rating. This requirement helps banks properly calculate risk weights under the Basel III Framework.

### 2.9 Know Your Customer (KYC)

KYC is a mandatory compliance process designed to verify a customer's identity before and during financial transactions. It serves as a frontline defense against Money Laundering (ML) and Terrorist Financing (TF).

* **IT System Focus:** Modern compliance relies heavily on **Centralized KYC (C-KYC)** databases, real-time risk-profiling scripts, and automated transaction monitoring algorithms that flag suspicious financial behavior (STRs/AMLs) directly out of Finacle or other Core Banking software.

### 2.10 Audit and Internal Control

These mechanisms ensure corporate governance, regulatory compliance, and system integrity.

* **Internal Audit:** An independent, objective assurance function within the bank that reviews operations, risk frameworks, and IT controls to report deficiencies directly to the Audit Committee.
* **External Audit:** A statutory inspection conducted annually by an independent chartered accountant to verify that the bank’s financial statements accurately reflect its true financial position.
* **Internal Control System:** Automated boundary checks embedded inside the bank's applications (such as split-maker-checker roles, dual approval rules, and database log integrity controls) designed to mitigate day-to-day operational errors or inside fraud.

### 3.1 Nepal Rastra Bank Act, 2058

The Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) Act, 2058 establishes NRB as an autonomous, corporate central bank. For an IT aspirant, understanding NRB’s core objectives regarding payment systems and its absolute regulatory authority over modern banking is crucial.

* **Objectives (Section 4):**
1. Maintain price, economic, and external sector stability to support sustainable economic growth.
2. Promote financial access and overall public confidence in the banking system.
3. Develop and manage a **secure, healthy, and efficient payment system** in Nepal.


* **Functions, Duties & Powers (Section 5):** Issue banknotes and coins, formulate and execute monetary/foreign exchange policies, license and regulate BFIs, act as the banker/advisor to the Government of Nepal, and operate as the **Lender of Last Resort** during systemic liquidity crises.
* **Autonomy:** The Act guarantees operational independence, shielding the Governor and Board from arbitrary removal except via formal, judicial inquiry committees on specific grounds (e.g., dishonesty or physical incapacity).

### 3.2 Bank and Financial Institution Act (BAFIA), 2073

BAFIA is the primary statute governing the lifecycle of all private and state-owned commercial banks (Class A), development banks (Class B), finance companies (Class C), and microfinances (Class D) in Nepal.

* **Establishment & Licensing:** Mandates that all BFIs must be registered as Public Limited Companies under the Companies Act and obtain a formal operational license from NRB under Section 34.
* **Corporate Governance (Chapter 4):** Restricts the Board of Directors (BoD) to 5 to 7 members. It strictly defines the **"Maker-Checker" relationship** between the Board (which handles policy) and the Chief Executive Officer (CEO, who handles day-to-day operations).
* **Permitted Transactions (Section 49):** Categorizes operations by class tier. Class "A" institutions like RBB have exclusive rights to provide wide-spectrum letter of credit (LC) instruments, international trade settlements, and specialized electronic fund transfers.

### 3.3 Payment and Settlement Act, 2075

This Act provides the legal foundation for all digital banking services, fintech operators, and electronic clearing clearings in Nepal.

* **Key Entities Defined:**
* **Payment Service Provider (PSP):** Entities licensed to operate digital wallets and process electronic transactions (e.g., eSewa, Khalti).
* **Payment System Operator (PSO):** Entities providing backend clearing infrastructure, transaction routing networks, and card payment switches (e.g., Nepal Clearing House Limited - NCHL).


* **Regulatory Framework:** Gives NRB absolute authority to manage, inspect, and suspend licenses of PSOs/PSPs. It grants statutory validity to electronic payment settlement systems like **RTGS** (Real Time Gross Settlement) and National Payment Switch (NPS) loops.

### 3.4 Money Laundering Prevention Act (ALMPA), 2064

ALMPA forms the legal basis for combating financial crimes, tax evasion, and the financing of terrorism. It mandates strict software-level controls within banking systems.

* **Core Obligations for Banks:**
1. **Customer Due Diligence (CDD/KYC):** Mandatory identification and continuous risk profiling of account owners.
2. **Reporting Requirements:** Banks must automatically capture and report **Threshold Transaction Reports (TTR)** (for cash transactions above 10 Lakh NPR) and **Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR)** regardless of amount, directly to the Financial Information Unit (FIU) of NRB.


* **Penalties:** Asset asset forfeiture, massive financial fines up to 5 times the laundered amount, and criminal imprisonment from 2 to 10 years for complicit bankers or entities.

### 3.5 Banking Offence and Punishment Act (BOPA), 2064

BOPA criminalizes fraudulent activities aimed at sabotaging banks or stealing client wealth, offering critical safeguards for electronic accounts.

* **Key Recognized Offences:**
* Opening bank accounts using fake or mismatched KYC documentation.
* **Cheque Dishonor (Check Bounce):** Deliberately issuing a check without sufficient funds.
* Unauthorized extraction of database records, hacking of core banking networks, altering credit parameters, or skimming automated teller machine (ATM) card details.


* **Punishment Matrix:** Strict asset recoveries (Bihgatti) matching the exact stolen amount, along with tiered imprisonment depending on the scale of financial damage.

### 3.6 Negotiable Instruments Act, 2034

This historic act establishes legal frameworks for corporate and retail financial instruments that pass value via endorsement.

* **Key Instruments Defined:** Promissory Notes (प्रतिज्ञा पत्र), Bills of Exchange (विनिमय पत्र), and Cheques (चेक).
* **Core Mechanics:** Outlines the legal definitions of a "Holder in Due Course," rules for instrument crossing (e.g., changing an open check to an "Account Payee Only" instrument to prevent unauthorized cash outs), and timelines for presenting or protesting dishonored bills.

### 3.7 Electronic Transaction Act (ETA), 2063

The ETA is the primary cyber law of Nepal. It validates all digital transactions executed across RBB networks.

* **Legal Recognition (Section 3 & 4):** Grants electronic records and digital signatures the exact same legal status, validity, and enforceability as physical, ink-signed paper documents.
* **Cybercrimes & IT Infractions (Chapter 9):**
* **Section 50:** Damage or deletion of computer source code (Fines up to 2 Lakh NPR and/or 3 years jail).
* **Section 51:** Unauthorized access to protected banking computers or core server infrastructures (Imprisonment up to 5 years).
* **Section 52:** Privacy breaches and unauthorized data leakage by privileged network administrators.

**Model Answer:**
The Banking Offence and Punishment Act (BOPA), 2064 protects digital infrastructure by legalizing financial cybersecurity enforcement. It criminalizes activities such as computer hacking, identity theft, and data manipulation.

Specifically, the Act:

1. **Criminalizes Unauthorized Access:** Section 6 makes it illegal to access a bank's computer systems, networks, or digital databases without explicit permission. This provides a clear path to prosecute both external hackers and unauthorized internal users.

### 4.1 Job Description, Terms of Reference (ToR), and Responsibilities

Clear documentation forms the baseline for administrative accountability and operational performance inside a banking institution.

* **Responsibilities (उत्तरदायित्व):** The moral and contractual obligation to execute assigned workflows efficiently. Within public banks, clear JDs and responsibilities reduce task duplication and minimize operational risks driven by human error.

1. **Forming:** Team members gather, establish baseline boundaries, and test out interpersonal dynamics.
2. **Storming:** Disagreements arise over project execution, personal work styles, and leadership roles.
3. **Norming:** The group resolves conflicts, harmonizes workflows, and establishes shared operational values.
4. **Performing:** The team operates at peak alignment, successfully shipping products and managing complex systems efficiently.


* **Workmanship (कार्यदक्षता):** Committing to high quality, operational precision, and ethical professional standards. In a banking environment, this is demonstrated by maintaining rigorous documentation, clean code repositories, and reliable backup habits.

* *Internal Customers:* Branch tellers, loan officers, and backend managers who rely on the IT department's applications to perform their daily duties. If internal IT support is slow, external client service suffers immediately.


* **Service Delivery Metrics:** Governed strictly by Service Level Agreements (SLAs), turnaround time (TAT) targets, and regular user experience (UX) quality audits.

### 6.1 Electronic Cheque Clearing (ECC)

**Electronic Cheque Clearing (ECC)** is an image-based check clearing system where digital images and magnetic ink character recognition (MICR) data of a physical check are captured and transmitted electronically from the presenting bank to the paying bank. This eliminates the need for physical movement of paper checks across banking counters.

* **Mechanism in Nepal:** Operated primarily by **Nepal Clearing House Limited (NCHL)** as NCHL-ECC.
* **Key Components:**
* **MICR Capture:** Reads the routing number, account number, and check number printed using magnetic ink.
* **Image Capture System (ICS):** Scans the front and back of the check to create high-resolution digital images.
* **Digital Signature:** Secures the image and data during transmission to prevent tampering.


* **Benefits:** Reduces the clearing cycle from days to hours, minimizes physical handling risks, and provides faster liquidity for customers.

---

### 6.2 InterBank Payment System (IPS), ConnectIPS, RTGS, and SWIFT

These systems constitute the primary core infrastructure for transferring funds both domestically and internationally.

```
+---------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                          PAYMENT SYSTEM INFRASTRUCTURE                          |
+---------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------+
|               DOMESTIC                |              INTERNATIONAL              |
+---------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------+
|  • NCHL-IPS (Deferred Net, Bulk)      |                                         |
|  • connectIPS (Real-time Retail)      |  • SWIFT (Global Messaging Network)     |
|  • RTGS (Real-time Gross Settlement)  |                                         |
+---------------------------------------+-----------------------------------------+

```

#### 1. InterBank Payment System (NCHL-IPS)

An interbank system designed for clearing and settling domestic bulk or individual account-to-account transactions.

* **Settlement Type:** Uses a **Deferred Net Settlement (DNS)** mechanism, where transactions are batched and settled periodically throughout the working day.
* **Use Cases:** Perfect for mass transactions like salary processing, government payments, dividend distributions, and IPO refunds.

#### 2. connectIPS

A fast-payment platform engineered by NCHL that allows low-value retail transactions to be initiated directly by bank customers.

* **Settlement Type:** Works on a **Real-Time Retail Payment (RRP)** engine, providing instant fund routing and processing 24/7/365.
* **Channels:** Accessible via a web portal, mobile app, or integrated directly via open APIs (National Payments Interface - NPI) into third-party channels like digital wallets.

#### 3. Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)

The high-value domestic payment infrastructure operated directly by the central bank (**Nepal Rastra Bank**).

* **Settlement Type:** **Real-time** (no waiting period or batch processing) and **Gross** (each transaction is processed individually).
* **Key Characteristics:** Transactions are final, unconditional, and irrevocable once settled. It is strictly used for high-value and systemically critical financial transactions (typically over NPR 2 Lakhs in Nepal).

#### 4. Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication (SWIFT)

A secure, global financial messaging network that enables financial institutions worldwide to send and receive information about financial transactions.

* **Crucial Distinction:** SWIFT **does not transfer funds** itself. Instead, it securely transmits standardized payment orders/messages (e.g., MT103 for single customer transfers) between correspondent banks across borders.

### 6.3 Internet Banking, Mobile Banking, QR Code, and NFC

#### Internet Banking & Mobile Banking

* **Internet Banking (E-Banking):** Accessing banking services through a secure web browser interface using a laptop or desktop. Provides a wider screen workspace suited for detailed balance reporting, large corporate transfers, and configuring recurring standing instructions.
* **Mobile Banking (M-Banking):** Utilizing a native smartphone application provided by a Bank and Financial Institution (BFI). Leverages device features like biometric authentication (fingerprint/face recognition) and push notifications for agile, on-the-go financial management.

#### Quick Response (QR) Code Payments

A two-dimensional matrix barcode containing structured merchant configuration data (such as merchant name, account identifier, and bank code).

* **Technical Standard:** Regulated under the **NepalQR Standardization Guidelines** issued by Nepal Rastra Bank.
* **Processing Ecosystem:** Retail payment applications scan the code to extract merchant parameters, passing the instruction to the National Payment Switch (NPS/RPS) to immediately initiate a push-based credit directly from the consumer's bank account or wallet to the merchant. In May 2026, cross-border QR payment connectivity was officially launched between Nepal and Sri Lanka (via connectIPS and LankaQR), demonstrating the regional interoperability of this tech.

#### Near Field Communication (NFC)

A short-range wireless communication technology operating at **13.56 MHz** that enables secure data exchange between two devices within a proximity of approximately 4 cm.

* **Application:** Enables "Tap-and-Pay" contactless payments. The consumer waves an NFC-enabled smartphone or contactless card over a compatible point-of-sale (POS) terminal, eliminating the need to insert cards or type PINs for low-value transactions.

### 6.4 Digital Payment Cards

Digital payment cards rely on standardized chip architectures and remote networks to securely authorize payments at point-of-sale (POS) terminals, e-commerce gateways, and Automated Teller Machines (ATMs).

#### Card Types by Funding Source

* **Debit Card:** Linked directly to the user's current or savings account. Transactions instantly debit the account balance.
* **Credit Card:** Employs a pre-approved revolving line of credit granted by the issuer. The user repays the accrued balance within a designated billing period.
* **Prepaid Card:** A card preloaded with a fixed monetary value. Funds are drawn from the loaded card balance rather than a live bank account.

#### Underlying Technologies

* **EMV Chip Technology:** Replaced legacy magnetic stripes with an embedded microprocessor chip complying with the Europay, Mastercard, and Visa (EMV) standard. It generates a unique, single-use cryptographic transaction code for every checkout, preventing card cloning.
* **Tokenization:** Swaps sensitive 16-digit primary account numbers (PAN) with a secure, randomized digital token. Even if intercepted by a malicious party during an e-commerce checkout, the token cannot be reused outside that specific transaction environment.
* **NEPALPAY Card:** Nepal's domestic card scheme launched under the National Payment Switch infrastructure to process transactions locally, reducing reliance on international card networks and lowering processing overhead for local BFIs.

### 6.5 Payment through Wallets

A **Digital Wallet (E-Wallet)** is a software application that securely stores users' payment credentials, linking them to an electronic value account or directly to integrated bank accounts.

* **Licensing Framework:** Regulated strictly by Nepal Rastra Bank under **Payment Service Provider (PSP)** frameworks.
* **Operational Models:**
* **Stored-Value Wallet:** Users preload the wallet account via mobile banking or linked debit cards before spending.
* **Bank-Linked Wallet:** Leverages open APIs to pull funds directly from a linked bank account at the moment of payment, eliminating the need to hold a resting balance in the wallet app itself.


* **Architecture & Interoperability:** Operates via secure APIs interacting with the Retail Payment Switch (RPS). It allows users to execute utility bill payments, person-to-person (P2P) transfers, and merchant checkouts using virtual payment addresses (VPAs) or mobile number identifiers.

### 6.6 Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)

A **Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC)** is a digital token representing the legal tender of a sovereign nation, issued and regulated directly by its central bank.

> **Key Distinction:** Unlike traditional digital balances (which represent a liability of a commercial bank) or cryptocurrencies (which are decentralized assets with highly volatile valuations), a CBDC constitutes a **direct liability of the Central Bank** and is backed 1:1 by sovereign reserves.

* **Core Architectural Formats:**
* **Retail CBDC:** Designed for everyday consumer-to-merchant (C2B) and peer-to-peer (P2P) transactions. It effectively acts as a direct digital replacement for physical paper banknotes.
* **Wholesale CBDC:** Restricts access to licensed financial institutions. It is optimized to settle high-value interbank settlements, clearing operations, and cross-border security transfers.


* **Technical Implementation:** Typically built on either highly optimized centralized ledgers or permissioned Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT). This balance ensures high transaction throughput while maintaining compliance with strict anti-money laundering (AML) and combatting the financing of terrorism (CFT) frameworks.
* **Context in Nepal:** Nepal Rastra Bank has systematically explored policy goals and structural designs through dedicated public consultation documents and pilot preparation roadmaps under its Strategic Plan to assess local financial stability, offline viability, and payment switch integrations.

## UNIT 2: Computer Architecture

### 2.1 Basic Computer Architecture & Memory Management

* **Von Neumann Architecture:** Features a unified memory space holding both program instructions and live operational data, accessed through a shared bus system.
* **Registers:** High-speed, volatile internal storage slots located inside the CPU core. Key registers include the **Program Counter (PC)**, **Instruction Register (IR)**, **Accumulator (AC)**, and **Memory Address Register (MAR)**.
* **Memory Management:** Implements **Paging** (splitting physical memory into fixed blocks called frames and logical memory into pages) and **Segmentation** (dividing memory into logical segments based on program structure) to support virtual memory paging and avoid physical memory conflicts.

### 2.2 Hard Disk Organization

Physical hard drives are structured into a precise geometric storage grid:

* **Platters:** Circular magnetic discs spinning around a central spindle.
* **Tracks:** Concentric magnetic rings written onto each platter surface.
* **Sectors:** Geometric divisions cut along each track; this is the smallest addressable unit of physical disk storage (traditionally 512 bytes).
* **Cylinders:** A vertical stack of identical track numbers aligned across all available platter layers.

```
       [   Hard Disk Geometry   ]
       
               /-------\         <- Platter
            /    /---\    \
           /    /     \    \     <- Track (Concentric Ring)
          |    |   o   |    |
           \    \     /    /     <- Sector (Pie slice of a track)
            \    \---/    /
               \-------/

```

### 2.3 CPU Architecture & Pipelining

The CPU executes instructions using a continuous instruction cycle: **Fetch $\rightarrow$ Decode $\rightarrow$ Execute $\rightarrow$ Writeback**.

* **Pipelining:** An optimization technique where multiple instructions are overlapped during execution. The pipeline architecture divides the execution cycle into distinct stages, allowing a new instruction to enter the first stage as soon as the previous one moves to the next. This increases overall instruction throughput without altering clock speed.

## UNIT 3: Communication and Computer Network Technologies

### 3.1 Networking Devices

* **Repeater (Layer 1):** Amplifies weak electrical signals to extend network cable transmission ranges. Does not filter data packets.
* **Hub (Layer 1):** A basic multi-port repeater. It broadcasts incoming data frames out to all connected ports, creating a shared collision domain.
* **Switch (Layer 2):** An intelligent multi-port device that filters and forwards data frames based on physical **MAC addresses**. It provides dedicated collision domains for each connected port.
* **Router (Layer 3):** An internetworking device that forwards data packets between separate logical networks using logical **IP addresses** and dynamic routing table paths.

### 3.2 Switching & Error Control

* **Circuit Switching:** Establishes a dedicated, physical end-to-end communication path between nodes before data transfer begins (e.g., traditional telephone lines).
* **Packet Switching:** Breaks data streams into discrete packets that travel independently across shared network pathways, dynamically routing through optimal links and reassembling at the destination.
* **Error Detection/Correction:** Includes techniques like **CRC (Cyclic Redundancy Check)** to detect bit errors via polynomial division, and **Hamming Code** algorithms to both detect and automatically correct single-bit errors using redundant parity bits.

### 3.3 Network Security & Cryptography

* **Symmetric Cryptography:** Uses a single, shared secret key for both encryption and decryption (e.g., AES, DES). It offers fast processing speeds but requires a secure channel for initial key distribution.
* **Asymmetric Cryptography:** Uses a mathematically linked key pair: a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption (e.g., RSA). This forms the core security framework for digital signatures and secure online banking portals (HTTPS).

## UNIT 4: Operating System and Information Systems

### 4.1 Basic Knowledge of Operating System (OS)

An **Operating System** is system software that acts as an intermediary interface between computer hardware and the user/application software, providing a stable platform for resource execution.

* **Core Functions of an OS:**
* *Processor Management:* Allocating CPU cycles to active tasks.
* *Memory Management:* Tracking RAM utilization, handling dynamic allocations, and managing virtual paging parameters.
* *File System Management:* Creating, deleting, organizing, and protecting directory structures.
* *Device/IO Management:* Interfacing with peripheral hardware via communication device drivers.
* *Security & Control:* Preventing unauthorized resource extraction and maintaining multi-user execution boundaries.



### 4.2 Process Management and Scheduling

* **Process:** A program in execution. Unlike a passive program code block, a process is an active entity containing a **Program Counter (PC)**, registers, and an isolated memory space.
* **Process Control Block (PCB):** A dedicated data structure in the OS kernel containing critical metadata for a specific process (e.g., Process ID, Process State, CPU Scheduling priority, Memory management pointers, and I/O status lists).
* **Process States:** Transitions through a defined life cycle: **New $\rightarrow$ Ready $\rightarrow$ Running $\rightarrow$ Waiting (Blocked) $\rightarrow$ Terminated**.
* **CPU Scheduling Algorithms:**
* *First-Come, First-Served (FCFS):* Non-preemptive; processes run in their arrival sequence. Vulnerable to the Convoy Effect (short jobs waiting behind a long-running process).
* *Shortest Job First (SJF):* Schedules the process with the shortest next CPU burst time. Proven to yield optimal average waiting times.
* *Round Robin (RR):* Preemptive, time-sliced algorithm designed for multi-user, time-sharing setups. Each process gets a fixed interval of execution time (Time Quantum).
* *Priority Scheduling:* Allocates CPU cycles based on an assigned integer value. Higher-priority processes take precedence, though lower-priority tasks risk starvation unless an aging technique is applied.



### 4.3 OS Ecosystems: DOS, UNIX, and Windows

* **DOS (Disk Operating System):** A classic 16-bit, single-user, single-tasking OS that utilizes a text-based Command Line Interface (CLI). It features a simple file architecture (`FAT12`/`FAT16`) and interacts directly with physical memory segments.
* **UNIX Operating System:** A multi-user, multi-tasking, network-centric OS built on a robust modular layout.
* *Architecture:* Divided into the **Kernel** (the core hardware abstraction layer) and the **Shell** (the command interpreter interface).
* *Banking Context:* Celebrated for its strict file-level permission matrices (`Read`, `Write`, `Execute`), making it an industry-standard platform for hosting core banking system (CBS) backends.


* **Windows Operating System:** A multi-user, multi-tasking OS featuring a Graphical User Interface (GUI) and built on a preemptive multitasking architecture. It uses the `NTFS` file system, which supports access control lists (ACLs), file-level encryption, and recovery logging for transaction consistency.

### 4.4 Identifying and Managing Security Threats in OS

* **Threat Identification Vectors:**
* *Privilege Escalation:* Exploits that allow standard system users to gain administrative root permissions.
* *Buffer Overflow Attacks:* Flooding input memory spaces to write malicious code execution vectors directly into adjacent execution logs.
* *Rootkits & Backdoors:* Stealthy programs that hide unauthorized system access mechanisms inside deep system layers.


* **OS Security Hardening Practices:**
* *The Principle of Least Privilege:* Restricting user accounts to the absolute minimum permissions needed to complete their assigned duties.
* *System Patching:* Applying security updates systematically to patch known CVE vulnerabilities.
* *Access Control Lists (ACLs):* Setting strict read/write permissions on critical system directories.
* *Kernel Log Auditing:* Actively tracking system calls and login failures via automated central logging setups.

## UNIT 5: Database Management System, Data Mining/Warehousing, and Web Technology

### 5.1 & 5.2 Database Architecture and Design Concepts

A **DBMS** is centralized system software used to store, retrieve, manipulate, and secure organizational data streams efficiently.

* **Core Structural Concepts:**
* *Table (Relation):* A structural matrix of rows and columns containing distinct data elements.
* *Record (Tuple/Row):* A complete, related set of field data representing a single entity instance within a table.
* *Field (Attribute/Column):* A single data category within a table.


* **Entity Relationships (ER):** Defines how distinct tables interact structurally: **One-to-One (1:1)**, **One-to-Many (1:M)**, or **Many-to-Many (M:N)**.
* **Indexing:** A performance optimization structure created on table columns to speed up data retrieval. It uses indexing trees (like B-Trees) to locate records quickly without scanning the entire table, which is essential for scaling high-volume banking transaction lookups.

### 5.3 Data Warehousing and Data Mining

* **Data Warehouse (DWH):** A centralized, historical repository that consolidates data from multiple transactional systems (like core banking databases and credit bureaus) to support management decision-making.
* **The ETL Process (Process in Data Warehousing):**
```
[ Operational OLTP ] --(Extract)--> [ Staging Area ] --(Transform)--> [ Load ] --> [ Target DWH ]

```


1. *Extraction:* Exporting raw data from various source databases.
2. *Transformation:* Standardizing, cleaning, resolving schema inconsistencies, and filtering out erroneous entries.
3. *Loading:* Writing the processed data into the central DWH repository for analytical analysis.


* **Data Mining:** The computerized process of extracting hidden, previously unknown patterns, correlations, and trends from large datasets using mathematical clustering and statistical algorithms.
* *Banking Use Case:* Identifying transaction fraud, profiling credit default risks, and forecasting customer retention patterns.



### 5.4 Web Technology and Infrastructure

* **HTML (HyperText Markup Language):** The foundational markup framework used to structure content displayed on web interfaces.
* **Scripts:** Specialized instructions used to add dynamic functionality to web systems. This includes client-side options like **JavaScript** (executed locally in the browser) and server-side alternatives like **PHP** or **Python** (executed on the host server).
* **Stylesheet (CSS):** Code files that manage presentation formatting, ensuring user-facing web applications maintain a clean layout.
* **XML (eXtensible Markup Language):** A text-based data format designed to structure and transmit information across web-based service systems.
* *Banking Value:* Often serves as the data transfer layer for connecting disparate APIs, clearing networks, and internal banking software components.


* **Web Server:** Software (e.g., Apache, Nginx) that uses HTTP/HTTPS protocols to process incoming requests from web browsers and serve the corresponding web page files.
* **Proxy Server:** An intermediary network server positioned between an internal workstation and the external internet. It hides internal IP structures, caches frequently requested web files to save bandwidth, and filters inbound traffic to block malicious external requests.

### 5.5 Disaster Recovery Planning (DRP)

A **Disaster Recovery Plan** is a documented, structured approach that outlines how a bank will restore its IT operations and safeguard data following a major system failure, natural disaster, or cyberattack.

* **Core Assessment Metrics:**
* **RTO (Recovery Time Objective):** The maximum allowable duration that a business function can remain unavailable before causing significant disruption.
* **RPO (Recovery Point Objective):** The maximum acceptable age of data that can be lost from backup storage due to a system interruption.



```
       [   The RTO and RPO Spectrum   ]
       
  <----- [ RPO ] ------------------------- | --------- [ RTO ] ----->
  (Last Safe Backup Point)           (Disaster Event)          (System Uptime Restored)

```

* **Backup and Recovery Models:**
* *Full Backup:* Captures a complete copy of all system data. It offers fast recovery speeds but requires substantial storage space and longer backup windows.
* *Differential Backup:* Copies only the data that has changed since the last full backup.
* *Incremental Backup:* Copies data that has changed since the most recent backup of any type, minimizing storage needs but requiring a multi-step restoration process.


* **Banking Redundancy Frameworks:**
* *Hot Site:* A fully operational backup facility that mirrors data in real-time, enabling near-instantaneous failover during a major outage.
* *Warm Site:* A backup facility equipped with the necessary hardware infrastructure but lacking real-time data synchronization, requiring recent backups to be restored before operations can resume.
* *Cold Site:* A designated facility with basic power and cooling infrastructure but no pre-installed hardware, serving as a long-term recovery option.
